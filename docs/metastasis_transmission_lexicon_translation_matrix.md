* **Gonopore Micrometastatic Shedding:** The periodic, pressurized release of microscopic larval blastemas, gametes, or cellular sub-units through specialized venting pores located on the coxal joints of the appendicular legs directly into host venous capillaries or mesenteric lymphatics.

* **Oviger-Mediated Dissemination:** The mechanical transport process where specialized appendicular limbs (ovigers) secure, shield, and carry developing micro-units along host fascial boundaries and deep connective tissue planes, accelerating multi-focal proliferation.

* **Vertical Trans-Chorionic Transit:** The physical migration mechanism where mobile blastemas penetrate the maternal chorioamniotic and placental barriers during gestational cycles, exposing a male or female fetus to direct, in utero viral and parasitic loads.

* **Lactiferous Fluid Micro-Venting:** The continuous leakage of active viral copy-numbers and fragmented lipid-chitin particles past thinning epithelial duct walls directly into lipid-rich maternal milk, generating an aggressive transmission vector.

* **Amniotic Fluid Saturation:** The progressive contamination of the protected intrauterine environment with low-pH cytolytic secretions and foreign reproductive fragments, leading to multi-systemic embryonic tissue degradation.

* **Anatomical Axis Migration:** The pathfinder routing of peripheral filamentous chains along long vascular or nervous pathways—such as the carotid transit line to the facial skeleton, or mesenteric networks to the descending colon—to seed new secondary vesicles.

* **Haustral Pouch Crowding:** The multi-focal multiplying of larvae within confined mucosal folds or glandular lobules, causing acute localized tissue displacement, luminal obstruction, and structural organ distortion.

* **Vascular Blastema Inundation:** A state of high-density systemic circulation where mobile larval sub-units overwhelm local host macrophage filters, leading to widespread multi-organ seeding.

* **Desmoplastic Core Replication:** The clonal expansion of a secondary colony following vascular transit, prompting adjacent host myofibroblasts to rapidly construct a new **Zone I External Rim** collagen barrier.

* **Embryonic Fluid Contamination Index:** A quantitative biochemical ratio tracking the concentration of foreign lipid-chitin remnants inside amniotic or trans-mammary fluid samples to measure vertical exposure vectors.
